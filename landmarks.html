Stonehenge

A prehistoric monument that was believed to be constructed around 4 thousand years ago (3000 to 2000 BC), according to archaeologists. Stonehenge is located on Salisbury Plain in Wiltshire, England, two miles west of Amesbury. The structure is consisting of an outer of vertical sarsen standing stones, standing around thirteen feet high, seven feet wide each. Each stone weighs around twenty-five tons, with a horizontal lintel stone on top of them. The ruined monument is aligned toward the sunrise on the summer solstice and is one of the most famous landmarks in the United Kingdom. Stonehenge has been regarded as a British cultural icon and has been a legally protected scheduled monument since 1882.


Discovery of deposits that contains human bones that date from as early as 3000 BC could indicate that Stonehenge could have been constructed as a burial ground. Stonehenge and its surrounding were added to the UNESCO's list of World Heritage Sites in 1986 and owned by the Crown. The site is managed by English Heritage and the surrounding land is owned by National Trust.
